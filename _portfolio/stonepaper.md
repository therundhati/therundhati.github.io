I pick a lifeless stone to pour in life with imagined birds, </br>
And ephemerality is the nemesis as in stone I set my words. </br>
Stone, that fragments scissors and all that crosses in its hour,  </br>
While all it takes is paper to stifle and wholly crush its power. </br>
I pick a lifeless paper, breaking hearts to make words bleed, </br>
For perhaps no resistance restrains my mind, to be freed. </br>
Paper when cannot dout, hurts with paper cuts unabashed, </br>
Till scissors leave it to be nothing but cursed confetti gashed. </br>
I pick scissors to end thought lives, to end a doomed existence, </br>
To end a trail of mindful steps, with much convinced insistence. </br>
And it’s all said and shred with rue, all that’s meant for shredding, </br>
It’s a stone that leaves it with no more than futile dreading. </br>
 </br>  </br> </br>
Scissors slash paper, paper throttles stone, </br>
Stone fragments scissors yet could I ever be alone, </br>
Nought fades warmth of smiles I carry, with love and cheer, </br>
Memories I anchor to, that my heart holds close and dear. </br>
And there’s no fire that could douse a cut, a tear, a lie, </br>
The imprints my mind endures, just another one more until I die. </br>
